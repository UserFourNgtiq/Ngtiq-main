What is DevOps

DevOps is a culture and a philosophy and not a highly prescriptive set of rules. The term “DevOps”was first coined by Patrick Debois in 2009, who became one of its primary proponents.

DevOps is a set of practices that combines software development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops). It aims to shorten the development life cycle and provide continuous delivery.

A philosophy for building and operating software, or infrastructure management that encourages teams to focus on business value, work collaboratively, and deploy software or solutions more frequently in smaller increments. DevOps promotes continuous improvement, and is a journey of perpetual refinement.

Benefits of DevOps

Foster agility through continuous deployment, testing, and feedback, allowing DevOps teams to build more apps and services at a higher velocity.

With highly automated configuration management and infrastructure, DevOps uses lightweight tools and automation to meet business demands and achieve faster delivery.

DevOps model facilitates easy communication and real-time feedback which ultimately results in removing development roadblocks. Collaboration between teams further streamlines workflows, reduces inefficiencies, and promotes accountability to deliver on time.>

Expedited product delivery and without any security vulnerability is possible because of automated operations and established security patterns in the DevOps lifecycle. Through automated secure processes, human errors are avoided and compliance is upheld.
